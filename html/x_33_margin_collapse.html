margin collapse
The top and bottom margins of blocks are sometimes combined (collapsed) into a single margin whose size is the largest of the individual margins (or just one of them, if they are equal), a behavior known as margin collapsing.
Note that the margins of floating and absolutely positioned elements never collapse.
Basic Cases
  1. Adjacent Siblings
    The top and bottom margins of adjacent siblings are collapsed
  2. Completely Empty Box
    If a box is empty, then its top and bottom margin may collapse with each other.
    Adding anything to the box, even margin-top or margin-bottom will prevent margin collapse

Rachel Andrew
When margins collapse, they will combine so that the space between the two elements becomes the larger of the two margins. The smaller margin essentially ending up inside the larger one. (see Things I Wish I’d Known About CSS )
display:grid and display:flex
Margins don’t collapse when the parent element is set to display: grid or display: flex. (Things I wish I'd known about CSS)
